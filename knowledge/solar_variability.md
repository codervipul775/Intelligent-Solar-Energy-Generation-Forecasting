# Solar Generation Variability and Risk Assessment

## Sources of Variability
Cloud transients cause rapid fluctuations (seconds to minutes) in solar generation, with drops of up to 80% possible within 30 seconds. Diurnal variation follows a predictable bell curve pattern but magnitude varies with season and latitude. Inter-day variability is driven by weather systems and can cause generation to vary by 50-80% between consecutive days.

## Risk Categories
**High Risk:** Generation drops below 20% of rated capacity during peak demand hours (4 PM - 8 PM). This requires immediate backup from spinning reserves or battery storage.

**Medium Risk:** Generation variability exceeds 30% within a 1-hour window. Grid operators should pre-position fast-response reserves and prepare demand response programs.

**Low Risk:** Generation follows expected seasonal patterns with minor deviations (<15%). Normal grid operations can accommodate this variability without special measures.

## Forecast Uncertainty
Day-ahead solar forecasts typically have Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of 10-20% of installed capacity. Intra-day forecasts (1-6 hours ahead) reduce MAE to 5-15%. Ensemble forecasting methods combining multiple models can reduce forecast error by 10-30% compared to single models.

## Seasonal Risk Patterns
Monsoon and rainy seasons present the highest risk for solar generation reliability, with daily generation potentially dropping by 60-80%. Winter months have shorter daylight hours but often have clearer skies, resulting in more predictable but lower total generation. Spring and autumn transition periods show highest inter-day variability as weather patterns shift.

## Mitigation Strategies
Maintain reserve margins of 15-25% of peak demand during high-variability periods. Deploy automated demand response that can shed 5-10% of load within 10 minutes. Use geographic diversity: solar installations spread across 50+ km can reduce aggregate variability by 50-70%. Implement ramp rate controls on large solar plants to limit output changes to 10% per minute.
