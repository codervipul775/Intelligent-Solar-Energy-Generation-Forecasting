# Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) for Solar Integration

## Storage Technologies
Lithium-ion batteries are the most widely deployed technology for grid-scale solar integration, offering round-trip efficiency of 85-95%. Flow batteries (vanadium redox) are suitable for long-duration storage (4-8 hours). Sodium-ion batteries are emerging as a cost-effective alternative for stationary storage applications.

## Sizing Guidelines
Battery storage capacity should be sized to cover 2-4 hours of peak solar generation for daily cycling applications. For grid reliability, storage should cover the maximum expected solar generation shortfall during peak demand hours. A typical rule of thumb is 25-30% of installed solar capacity for co-located storage.

## Charging Strategy
Batteries should charge during peak solar generation hours (typically 10 AM - 2 PM) when generation exceeds demand. Charging rate should not exceed the C/2 rate to maximize battery life (e.g., a 100 kWh battery should charge at no more than 50 kW). State of charge should be maintained between 20% and 80% for optimal battery longevity.

## Discharging Strategy
Discharge during evening peak demand hours (typically 5 PM - 9 PM) when solar generation drops to zero. Reserve 10-15% of capacity for emergency grid support and frequency regulation. Prioritize discharge during highest electricity price periods to maximize economic value.

## Degradation Management
Lithium-ion batteries typically lose 2-3% capacity per year under normal cycling conditions. Depth of discharge, temperature, and charge rate are the primary factors affecting degradation. Battery management systems (BMS) should monitor cell voltages, temperatures, and state of health continuously.

## Economic Considerations
Battery storage becomes economically viable when the price differential between peak and off-peak electricity exceeds the levelized cost of storage. Revenue streams include energy arbitrage, frequency regulation, capacity payments, and demand charge reduction. Typical payback periods for commercial solar-plus-storage installations range from 5-8 years.
